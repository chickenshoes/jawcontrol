02 Audio Source
Only applies to the file-playback audio above. The built-in TTS voice (panel 03) plays through your OS's default output device — browsers don't currently allow picking a device for it via code. Chrome/Edge only. If you're missing devices (e.g. monitor speakers, a virtual cable), click Show all devices — Chrome hides most output devices and their real names until a page has been granted microphone access at least once, even though none of your audio is actually recorded or sent anywhere.
03 Text-to-Speech (free, built-in)
Uses your browser/OS's built-in speech synthesis - no API key, no cost, works offline. Since it doesn't expose raw audio samples, the jaw is driven by word-boundary timing (opens per word, decays between) rather than true volume - looks convincing in practice, just isn't literally amplitude-based like the file-upload path above. It's also timer-based rather than audio-thread-based, so unlike file playback, movement may lag if you switch away from this tab mid-sentence.
04 Discord Bridge (auto-speak)
Not connected
Connects to the separate Discord bridge script (discord-bridge/index.js) running locally. When it detects someone joining a voice channel, it sends a message here and the head speaks automatically using the voice/settings above - no button press needed.
